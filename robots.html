Robots!


2014 2013 2012 2011

2014 | Dark Matter

Our 2014 robot Dark Matter, successfully took first place in various competitions. Dark Matter was equiped with a 6 motor tank drive train. A powerful catapult with a 180 degree intake for faster loading. Programmed for on the fly angle adjusting as well as sequences allowing for fast actions in the playing field. Build to run a two ball autonomous mode using various sensors throughout the robot. We successfully took first place at Rhode Island District Event, Northeastern University District Event, Pine tree District Event and even ranked 7 in curie division. Ranked 19 in FRC Top 25

2013 | Fission

Our 2013 robot, Fission. It won first place in the Boston Regional, while coming in as a finalist at the Orlando regional in Florida, and the Pine Tree regional at Maine. It features a consistent three disk autonomous mode, and an extremely accurate shooter. It can intake off the ground, or from the middle human loader. It shoots in the high goal, at the back middle of the pyramid, or at a front corner using its elevator

2012 | Fusion

Our 2012 robot, Fusion, was the New York Regional champion and Boston Regional finalists, while also making it to IRI. It could consistently make two shots in the high goal in autonomous. This robot featured an effective human loader, as well as a proficient ground intake. We had an accurate shooter, excelling in hitting the high goal. It also would feed the alliances friendly side by shooting balls into it so our teammates could score points.

2011 | Firefly

Our 2011 robot, Firefly, was a finalist at the Boston and Bayou Regionals. It featured one of the fastest mini-bots at each competition. It had a very fast and accurate elevator, at the end of which was a rollerclaw which sucked in tubes. Twice during the season, it managed to get a two uber-tube autonomous. This was the first year we were invited to IRI.
